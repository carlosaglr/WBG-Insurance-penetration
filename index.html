Insurance ·

Argentina vs Bolivia · structural contrast

Two regulator files reported so far. SSN (Argentina) reports a commercial insurance market. INSA (Bolivia) reports a state-subsidized catastrophic program (Pachamama MINKA) plus a thin commercial trigo product. The numbers reflect that difference more than scale.

Why Bolivia's numbers are not Argentina's numbers ÷ 100
Argentina's market is voluntary and commercial. Producers pay premiums, insurers (Sancor, Allianz, Segunda) compete on price and cover, and the SSN reports premium volumes, claims, and a financial loss ratio in pesos. Bolivia operates two parallel layers: (1) the state-subsidized catastrophic MINKA via INSA for smallholders in poor municipios, no producer-side premium but the largest by hectares and claims paid; and (2) a thin commercial line via APS / UNIBIENES, 9 approved products, $0.2M USD premium in 2024 vs Argentina's $232M. The take-up gap (43% vs 3%) reflects this structural split, not a quality gap.
